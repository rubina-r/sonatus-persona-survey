Ian "Loop Master" Lorenz
Pre-SOP Integration & Validation Engineer
"I manage a fleet of Frankenstein prototypes. When the vehicle is sitting in the pit lane between runs, I have maybe four minutes to push a fix, confirm it landed, and get the driver back on track. If the cloud console can't keep up with that reality, I'm rolling the car back to the garage for a hardwired hookup."
Role & context
Owns the end-to-end software integration loop for pre-production prototype vehicles on test tracks and in HIL/SIL labs. Bridges cloud deployment and physical automotive hardware across mismatched ECU generations, Central Gateways, and Zonal Controllers that are never on the same firmware version.
How they use Sonatus
UpdaterGranular per-ECU flashing and brute-force baseline campaigns
Collector AIBlack-box diagnostics and high-fidelity fault capture
FoundationNetwork policy overrides and routing configuration
⚙Environmental context - the UX reality check
▣ The hardware chaos
He rarely flashes a uniform software version. Central Gateway runs today's alpha; the Zonal Controller is three months behind; a critical brake ECU is a physical bypass module with legacy firmware. Every vehicle is a unique configuration.
⛰ The physical environment
Noise, grease, glare-heavy screens on a steering wheel laptop, spotty 4G/5G on track. He is never at a desk.
⚡ Power state panic
Vehicles aggressively drop to Deep Sleep to save the 12V battery, or a mechanic cycles ignition mid-test. An interrupted OTA flash can brick an ECU worth more than a car.
🎯 Jobs to be done
Primary jobs - core progress
1Stage the vehicle baselinePrimary
When I am preparing a prototype fleet for a track day,
I want to reliably flash and configure mismatched hardware/software components across the vehicle network,
So that the vehicle is in a known, predictable state and doesn't brick before it hits the tarmac.
2Capture low-latency, high-fidelity fault dataPrimary
When an intermittent anomaly or network error occurs during a high-speed test run,
I want to automatically clip and isolate a precise window of raw network traffic pre- and post-event,
So that I have microsecond-accurate diagnostic proof of what caused the failure.
3Rapidly iterate configurations between test runsPrimary
When a test run fails due to a network routing or policy issue,
I want to push a lightweight OTA fix or logging policy update while the car is idling in the pit lane,
So that we don't waste expensive track time or roll the car back to the garage for a hardwired hookup.
4Verify physical state vs. digital claimsPrimary
When the cloud console reports an update campaign as "Success",
I want to audit raw hardware registers and ECU data identifiers (DIDs) directly from the vehicle bus,
So that I can confidently sign off on vehicle safety before handing the keys to a test driver.
Secondary jobs - enablers & insurance
5Arbitrate supplier blameSecondary
When a Tier 1 supplier claims their component failed because of a gateway lag or network conflict,
I want to pull deterministic network bus logs,
So that I can pinpoint root cause and prove where the liability lies.
6Support OEM onboarding configurationSecondary
When a new OEM team is setting up their first Sonatus-managed vehicle,
I want to respond quickly to their configuration change requests and push remote fixes without being on-site,
So that their initial integration runs complete without requiring a physical lab visit.

Sam "Cycle Time" Suzuki
SOP · Factory-Floor Launch & End-of-Line (EOL) Engineer
"If a vehicle takes 10 seconds too long to register on the cloud or complete its factory test loop, I hold up the entire assembly line. The line supervisor is standing two meters behind me. Seconds cost millions here - and every second is visible."
Role & context
Owns the execution of software deployment and end-of-line verification as vehicles transition from a skeleton of parts to an initialized, rolling product on the factory floor. Operates under strict takt time - a new car rolls off every 60–90 seconds at peak. There is no room for slow APIs, confusing UI spinners, or manual troubleshooting.
How they use Sonatus
Automator AIFactory diagnostic scripts and automated provisioning workflows
FoundationSecurity credential provisioning and identity management
UpdaterFirst factory flash verification and software baseline confirmation
⚙Environmental context - the UX reality check
▤ Assembly line cadence
Vehicles move on strict takt times. A new car reaches Sam's station every 60–90 seconds. There is zero room for slow APIs, confusing spinners, or manual troubleshooting. A blocked station backs up every vehicle behind it.
✕ Factory network constraints
Factory floors are massive Faraday cages. Wi-Fi drops are common and the local assembly network is locked down by rigid IT restrictions. Cloud connectivity is unreliable - local-first execution is not optional.
⌛ High-stakes compliance
Every car must legally conform to a strict software bill-of-materials. An ECU with an unverified or mismatched software signature cannot leave the factory. Compliance failures flag an entire production batch.
🎯 Jobs to be done
Primary jobs - core progress
1Bulk-provision security & network identitiesPrimary
When I am initializing thousands of newly assembled vehicles per day,
I want to instantly and automatically provision unique vehicle tokens, security credentials, and baseline routing tables,
So that each car securely wakes up and registers to the OEM cloud database without manual technician intervention.
2Execute rapid end-of-line (EOL) diagnosticsPrimary
When a vehicle reaches the final quality checkpoint on the factory floor,
I want to run a highly optimized, automated test script across all vehicle buses via local diagnostic commands,
So that I can instantly confirm all ECUs are alive, communicating, and error-free before the car rolls into the shipping yard.
3Recover a failed EOL vehicle without stopping the linePrimary
When a vehicle fails an EOL diagnostic check and the assembly line is backing up behind it,
I want to instantly triage the failure, identify whether it is a software or hardware fault, and push a targeted remote fix,
So that the vehicle clears the station within minutes and the line keeps moving without manual rollback to a repair bay.
Secondary jobs - enablers & insurance
4Audit software bills-of-materialsSecondary
When a vehicle is flagged by quality control for a configuration mismatch,
I want to instantly verify its current software versions against the approved digital twin blueprint,
So that I can safely flash the correct baseline without manually plugging in a physical OBD-II diagnostic tool.

Fiona "Fleet-Wide" Foster
Post-SOP · Fleet Performance & Connected Vehicle Reliability Engineer
"I oversee large-scale production fleets in the real world. A single bad cloud configuration or unoptimized telemetry policy can cause thousands of customer complaints or rack up millions in cellular data overages. I don't get to test in a lab - my lab is live traffic."
Role & context
Manages the health, software lifecycle, and data optimization of consumer or commercial vehicles after they've been sold and are actively on public roads. Operates at fleet scale - her decisions affect vehicle cohorts, not individual units. Bridges operational monitoring with data investigation to isolate systemic issues before they become recalls.
How they use Sonatus
AI TechnicianFleet diagnostics, automated anomaly detection, and log analysis
Collector AICost-optimized, trigger-based data collection policies
UpdaterMass OTA safety campaigns and staged rollout management
⚙Environmental context - the UX reality check
▤ Aggregated data deluge
She isn't looking at one car - she's looking at graphs representing millions of operational hours. UI lag from massive data loading will ruin her experience. Every second of latency costs confidence in the data.
🛡 The scary deployments
Pushing a software update or changing a gateway routing policy to a live fleet of customer-owned vehicles carries severe safety and brand liability. One mistake can trigger a recall. She needs staged rollout and instant rollback - not optimism.
📲 Cellular bill pressures
Cellular data isn't free at scale. A policy that forces vehicles to upload uncompressed logs over LTE can blow operational budgets in days. Data efficiency is a financial control she is accountable for.
🎯 Jobs to be done
Primary jobs - core progress
1Proactively isolate fleet-wide anomaliesPrimary
When I am monitoring large-scale production vehicle fleets,
I want to leverage AI-driven diagnostics to detect emerging patterns of intermittent hardware or software faults across specific vehicle cohorts,
So that we can resolve the root cause before it turns into a safety hazard or a costly physical recall.
2Deploy safe, tiered mass OTA updatesPrimary
When a critical software patch must be distributed to the global vehicle fleet,
I want to configure a controlled, staged deployment roll-out (e.g. 1% → 5% → 25% → 100%) with automated rollback triggers,
So that we mitigate the risk of an unforeseen software bug bricking thousands of customer cars in the field.
Secondary jobs - enablers & insurance
3Enforce cost-optimized data collectionSecondary
When engineering asks for deep diagnostic telemetry to investigate a field issue,
I want to build a highly targeted, trigger-based collection policy that filters and compresses data at the vehicle edge,
So that I give engineers the exact data they need without paying for massive cellular carrier overage fees.
4Report fleet health to leadership and safety teamsSecondary
When leadership or a safety review board needs a status update on fleet software health,
I want to generate a clear, exportable summary of fleet-wide update compliance, open anomalies, and recent deployment outcomes,
So that I can communicate risk and progress without manually assembling data from multiple dashboards.
